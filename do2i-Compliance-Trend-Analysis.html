Definitions

  • DO2i - measure of delivery of oxygen mL/min/m^2
  • DO2i272 - whether or not a particular DO2i reading (one of multiple in a case) was above 272 and thus compliant
  • AUC632_DO2i272 - total “area under the curve” for a case, using DO2i of 272 as cutoff for starting to measure oxygen debt
  • COR_DO2_TS.csv - data set containing individual DO2i readings for cases under analysis
  • COR_DO2.csv - data set containing calculated AUC_DO2i272 readings, one per case. This is the total AUC for each case when using 272ml/min/m^2 as the cutoff for beginning to accululate area under the curve.

Interpretation

We analyzed 30,101 DO2i observations from 5,325 cases reporting at least one DO2i observation per case among non-reopeartive CABGs between 2022-06-01 and 2022-12-31. The median number of observations per case was 5, the mean was 5.6506476, with a standard deviation of 3.3429817. There was no trend over the study period in the number of Do2i observations collected per case. Compliance to DO2i practice guidelines was defined as a DO2i of 272 or greater. Binary logistic regression models were estimated to quantify the magnitude and statistical uncertainty of the apparent upward trend in probability of compliance over the study period in individual DO2i readings as well as area under the curve, using the threshold of 272 and an overall AUC of 632. Time was operationalized as the week of year in which a case and it’s accompanying observations was completed, between 22 and 52. Results from this model indicate a statistically significant linear increase in the log odds/predicted probability of compliance to the 272 threshold (p<0.001). A similar analysis of AUC compliance was carried out on 5,292 cases with at least two DO2i readings over the same time period. Logistic regression analysis showed a statistically reliable positive trend over the study period in the probability of compliance to an AUC of 632 (p<0.001).
